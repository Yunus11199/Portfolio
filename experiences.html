My Experiences

Queen Mary University of London

  • Course Representative (Robotics Engineering)

    • I was the elected course representative for student on my course. During my time as course representative, I seeked out students' views and represented them at Staff Student Liaison Committee (SSLC) meetings, working in partnership with staff to improve our course and resolve any course-related issues as they arise throughout the year.

    I also received training to be an effective and supportive course representative

  • Welfare Officer (Science and Engineering)

    • As a Wefare Officer, I engaged with and lobbied Queen Mary University of London and other relevant organisations to achieve improvements for students on matters relating to student welfare. I also sought feedback from Science and Engineering (S&E) students in order to accurately represent their interests. In addition, I supported the Executive Officers in their duties and responsibilities, coordinated and collaborated with the Vice President of Welfare, the Welfare Officer for Barts and the London (BL) and the Welfare Officer for Humanities and Social Sciences (HSS) as well as liaise with members of student groups.

    I attended meetings with the Student Council, the Science and Engineering Board and the Welfare Board.

  • Welfare and Safety Officer (Archery Club)

    I ensured the welfare of club members was looked after and that members were safe during their time at the Archery Club.

    I undertook relevant training in regards to welfare such as inclusion, promoting diversity and how to deal with reports of sexual harrassment.
